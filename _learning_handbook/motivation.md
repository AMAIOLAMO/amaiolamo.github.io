[Motivation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motivation) is a pretty interesting topic as it is one of the extremely big factors of traditional success.
It drives us forward in seemingly the most hardest situations...
But on the other hand, there are many instances in life where you get demotivated, especially when you don't see any progress,
feel burnt-out or feel like you did not get the "expected" reward when you accomplished certain things associated to it.

#### Motivation After Doing



#### Draft your Motivational Chain
Most often than not, the underlying motivation of a thing you wish to do is hidden under layers and layers of "wants" and usually they can be represented as a "chain".

It is as if someone continuously ask you about the seemingly innocent question: "why?" when you are explaining your reason of motivation towards a specific goal.

For instance:

"I like programming"

"Why?"

"Because I wanna prove that I am smart"

"Why?"

"To increase my confidence"

"Why?"

"To feel like I've been seen by others and feel useful"

Usually, this "conversation" ends with primitive emotions and basic necessities, to feel happy, to feel wanted, to feel safe etc. etc

A long motivation chain can make you confused and feel like you like the thing but also not really like the thing you wanted to do. You may like the result of drawing, but cant start drawing yourself.

Hence shortening the motivation chain to DIRECTLY point towards the source / root of motivation is the key. And that's why drawing out the motivational chain is useful. It helps you identify what can be worked on to improve what you really want.
