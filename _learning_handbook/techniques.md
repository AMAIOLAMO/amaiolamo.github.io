<summary><a href="https://sites.lsa.umich.edu/equitable-teaching/wp-content/uploads/sites/853/2020/09/Question-Formulation-Technique-Draft-3.pdf">Question Formulation Technique(QFT)</a>: This is quoted directly from the link</summary>
<div markdown=1>
```
1) Ask as many questions as you can.
2) Do not stop to discuss, judge, or answer any of the questions.
3) Write down every question exactly as it was stated.
4) Change any statements into questions.
```
</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RDzsrmMl48I">Blackbox Method</a>: Learn how to use first, before learning how it works</summary>

<div markdown=1>
This method urges you to first learn how a thing is used, before you start delving into the deep nested logic on how a certain concept works. It does not serve as a one-in-all technique, but rather simply just a starting point for any kind of thing you wished to learn.
```
Here are the steps that I have summarized from the video for the method:

1) Have a concept A that you wished to learn.
2) Think of A as a blackbox(or a machine) that has some "input" and "output".
3) Study and practice how to use this blackbox and hold yourself before you even study how it all works.
4) While studying and practicing, only focus the overall picture of the blackbox, and ignore how it works.
5) Simplify the concepts without going too deep.
```
</div>
</details>
