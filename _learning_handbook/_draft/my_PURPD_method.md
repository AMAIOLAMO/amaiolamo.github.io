Throughout my experiences in High school and University, I studied how to learn (which is why you have this learning handbook in the first place),
and wished to have a step by step process in making learning much more systematic :). I always have this chaotic system of trying things out whenever I feel like it.
I do not like it though, so that's why I invented my own system called **PURPD**.

#### What is "PURPD"?

PURPD is an acronym that stands for:
- Plan Short
- Understand Broad / Deep
- Recall Actively
- Practice Efficiently
- Document Reflection

PURPD is meant to be done in very short bursts of duration, similar to the Pomodoro Timer technique, you use one sheet of paper for this specific purpose.

This is why the "**P**" stands for "**Plan Short**", to plan something you will try to achieve one tiny step at a time. This technique is inspired
by [agile development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agile_software_development) process in programming.


#### Scope of PURPD

Of course, all techniques has it's flaws, PURPD only accounts for situations that are more on theoretical studies.
This is why it's done mostly on a piece of paper.
